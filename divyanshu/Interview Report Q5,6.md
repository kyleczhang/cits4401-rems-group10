## Interview Report Q5,6

### Interview Questions Covered

**Q5. How do you communicate with researchers when clarifications or revisions are needed?**

**Q6. What are the most common reasons applications are delayed or returned to researchers?**

---

### Response Summary and Analysis

One important clarification from the interview was how communication with researchers currently takes place when clarification or revision is needed. The interviewee explained that communication is mainly conducted through email, but the type of issue determines who communicates with the researcher. Administrative staff contact researchers when documents are missing or when there are inconsistencies in the submission. However, administrative staff do not have access to the contents of submitted documents. They can only see file names or document headers. If the issue relates to the actual content of a document, the Ethics Committee communicates directly with the researcher. This shows that the current process already has a clear separation between logistical communication and content-related communication.

Another key insight from the interview was the cause of delays in the ethics approval process. The interviewee explained that delays occur both at the submission stage and at the review stage. Submission-stage delays happen when researchers do not fully understand which supporting documents are required, or when some documents are missing. In some cases, language barriers may also make the submission requirements harder to understand. Review-stage delays happen when all required documents are present, but the contents within those documents are not sufficient for approval. This distinction is important because administrative staff only verify whether the required documents are present, while the Ethics Committee is responsible for assessing the actual content and quality of the documents.

These answers changed our understanding in an important way. Initially, communication and delays could have been seen as one general workflow problem. After the interview, it became clear that the workflow should be understood in two parts: first, an administrative process focused on document presence and completeness, and second, a committee review process focused on the quality and adequacy of document content. This means the new REMS should support different communication paths, different levels of access, and clearer status tracking across both stages of the application process.

---

### Key Insights

- Communication with researchers is mainly email-based.
- Administrative staff handle logistical issues such as missing documents.
- Ethics Committee members handle content-related clarifications directly.
- Delays occur at both submission and review stages, but for different reasons.
- Administrative staff verify document presence, while the committee evaluates content quality.

---

### Impact on Requirements and Refinements

- The system should support separate communication paths for logistical and content-related issues.
- Communication records should be attached to the application record for traceability.
- The system should provide clearer submission guidance to reduce missing-document delays.
- The system should support status visibility across both submission review and committee review.
- Role-based access control should reflect the distinction between admin-side checks and committee-side content review.
